### Region 3 — Typical PCBA Project Path

1. **Receive files and requirements** — Gerber or ODB++, BOM, CPL, assembly drawing, quantity and testing needs.
2. **Review BOM and manufacturability** — Identify missing data, availability, lifecycle, MOQ, approved-alternative and DFM/DFA questions.
3. **Coordinate PCB fabrication when included** — Confirm stack-up, material, surface finish, panelization and assembly-related board requirements.
4. **Prepare PCB Assembly** — Define SMT, through-hole or mixed assembly, special handling and first-article expectations.
5. **Apply agreed inspection and testing** — Confirm the relevant process checks, fixtures, firmware, procedures and acceptance criteria.
6. **Complete packaging and delivery requirements** — Confirm labeling, records, handling and shipment details included in the quotation.

### Region 6 — Capability and Quality Evidence

- **H2:** How are assembly, inspection and testing requirements confirmed?

1. **Revision alignment:** The current Gerber, BOM, CPL and drawings should describe the same product revision.
2. **BOM and approved alternatives:** Availability or lifecycle issues are reviewed before any customer-approved substitution is used.
3. **Assembly-process planning:** Component orientation, package, special handling and mixed-process requirements are reviewed from the files.
4. **Inspection checkpoints:** FAI, SPI, AOI, visual inspection or X-ray may be discussed according to board and package risk.
5. **Electrical or functional testing:** ICT, FCT or product-specific checks require an agreed method, fixture, firmware and acceptance criteria.
6. **Records and packaging:** Required reports, labels, traceability depth and handling instructions should be stated before production.

## FAQ

### What is the difference between PCB Assembly and PCBA?
PCB Assembly is the commonly understood service name. PCBA is the abbreviation for Printed Circuit Board Assembly and is widely used for both the assembled board and the assembly process.

### What does Turnkey PCBA include?
A turnkey scope may coordinate PCB fabrication support, BOM review, component sourcing, assembly, inspection, project-specific testing and packaging. The quotation defines which activities are included.

### Can I supply my own components?
Yes. Consigned or partial-turnkey assembly can be discussed. The project should define supplied quantities, incoming inspection, shortages and responsibility for nonconforming material.

### Can Venture suggest alternative components?
Venture can review availability and lifecycle risks and discuss candidate alternatives. No alternative should be used until the buyer reviews and approves it.

### What testing can be included?
Relevant inspection, ICT, FCT or product-specific functional checks can be discussed. Coverage depends on board design, test points, fixtures, firmware, samples and acceptance criteria.

### How are MOQ and lead time determined?
Both are reviewed from the project files, BOM, quantity, assembly process, testing requirements and material availability. Venture does not state one universal schedule or quantity rule for every project.

### Can a prototype move into repeat production?
Prototype feedback can support later production planning when the design, BOM, test requirements and approved revisions become stable. Repeat production still requires scope, material and quality review.

### Region 2 — EMS / Box Build Flow

- **H2:** How can a project move from PCBA into Box Build?

PCBA → Board-level testing → Final assembly → Box Build → System checks → Packaging and delivery coordination

The exact path is confirmed from the mechanical files, system BOM, assembly instructions, test plan, packaging requirements and acceptance criteria. Not every PCBA order includes all of these stages.

### Region 3 — Scope Categories

- **H2:** What can an EMS or Box Build scope include?

1. **PCBA integration** — approved boards assembled with enclosure, cabinet, connectors or mechanical components.
2. **Cable or harness discussion** — wire, connector, routing and inspection requirements when drawings and specifications are available.
3. **System-level checks** — functional or interface checks based on customer procedures, fixtures, firmware and acceptance criteria.
4. **Identification and packaging** — labels, serial numbers, packaging materials, accessories and shipment preparation when included in the quotation.

Software installation, RF, safety, regulatory, retail-packaging or fulfillment requirements must be stated explicitly so Venture can confirm whether they fit the project.

### Region 4 — Required Inputs

- Approved PCBA files and board revision
- Complete system BOM, including electronic, cable, mechanical and packaging items
- Enclosure and mechanical drawings
- Cable or harness drawings and connector requirements
- Assembly sequence, torque or fastening notes where applicable
- Firmware, configuration or programming requirements
- System-level test steps, fixtures, samples and acceptance criteria
- Label, serial-number, packaging and delivery instructions

### Region 5 — NPI to Repeat Production

- **H2:** What should be stabilized before an EMS project repeats?

1. **Scope clarification:** define the product form, PCBA status, enclosure, harness, test and packaging needs.
2. **Engineering review:** identify electronic, mechanical, cable and test-interface risks.
3. **BOM and supply review:** check availability, lifecycle, MOQ and approved alternatives across the system BOM.
4. **Prototype or pilot build:** verify assembly order, fixtures, functional checks, labels and packaging.
5. **Repeat-production preparation:** freeze approved revisions, material rules, assembly instructions, test requirements and records.

### Region 6 — System Testing and Packaging

- **H2:** How are system checks and packaging requirements defined?

System-level testing differs from board-level PCBA testing because it may involve the assembled enclosure, cable connections, power, interfaces, indicators or product functions. Buyers should provide the test procedure, firmware or software, fixtures, golden sample and acceptance criteria where available.

Packaging requirements should identify protection, accessories, labels, serial numbers, manuals, destination and shipment documentation. Retail-ready packaging, global fulfillment or regulatory labeling should not be assumed unless the quotation specifically confirms them.

## FAQ

### What is the difference between PCBA and Box Build?
PCBA is the assembled circuit board. Box Build combines an approved PCBA with additional parts such as an enclosure, cable, connectors, mechanical components, labels, system checks or packaging.

### Can Venture assemble products using my enclosure and mechanical parts?
That scope can be discussed when mechanical drawings, parts, tolerances, fastening instructions and acceptance criteria are available for review.

### Can cable or wire-harness work be included?
Cable or harness work can be reviewed when drawings, connector specifications, wire requirements, routing and inspection expectations are provided. The quotation confirms whether it is included.

### Can system-level functional testing be included?
Yes, when the customer provides or approves the test method, fixtures, firmware, samples and acceptance criteria. Coverage is project-specific.

### Does Venture provide retail-ready packaging and global fulfillment?
Packaging and delivery preparation can be discussed, but retail packaging, regulatory labeling and fulfillment should be treated as separate project requirements and confirmed in writing.

### Can an EMS project move from prototype to repeat production?
A pilot build can help stabilize the product revision, system BOM, assembly instructions, test process and packaging. Repeat production still depends on approved files, material availability and the agreed quality plan.

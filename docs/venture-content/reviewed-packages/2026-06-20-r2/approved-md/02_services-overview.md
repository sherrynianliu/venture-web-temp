### Region 2 — Core Service Cards

1. **PCB Assembly / PCBA**
   Choose this route when the main deliverable is an assembled circuit board. The page explains consigned, partial-turnkey and full-turnkey discussion, assembly scope, RFQ files and project-specific testing.

2. **EMS & Box Build**
   Choose this route when approved PCBAs need to be combined with enclosure, cable or harness, mechanical parts, system checks, labeling or packaging.

3. **Component Sourcing, BOM & DFM Review**
   Use this supporting route to address BOM completeness, availability, lifecycle, MOQ, approved alternatives and manufacturability questions before procurement or assembly.

4. **PCB Fabrication Support**
   Use this route to align Gerber or ODB++, stack-up, material, copper, impedance, finish and panelization requirements with downstream assembly.

### Region 3 — How the Services Connect

- **H2:** How do Venture Electronics services connect within one project?

1. **Start with the intended deliverable.** An assembled board begins with PCB Assembly / PCBA; a more complete module or product may require EMS & Box Build.
2. **Define the files and supply model.** Gerber, BOM, CPL, drawings, quantities and sourcing responsibilities shape the quotation.
3. **Resolve engineering and supply-chain questions.** BOM review, approved alternatives, DFM/DFA and fabrication inputs should be settled before production.
4. **Confirm inspection, testing and delivery requirements.** The quotation should state the agreed checks, records, packaging and delivery scope.

### Region 4 — Project Review Inputs

- **H2:** What should buyers prepare before selecting a final service scope?

- Gerber or ODB++ files
- BOM with MPN, manufacturer, quantity and substitution rules
- CPL / pick-and-place data
- Assembly drawings, polarity notes and mechanical requirements
- Quantity and target schedule
- Test plan, fixture, firmware, golden sample or acceptance criteria when available
- Packaging, labeling and delivery requirements

The file set does not need to be perfect before the first conversation, but missing information may limit quotation accuracy or require follow-up.

## FAQ

### Which service should I choose if I only need assembled circuit boards?
Start with PCB Assembly / PCBA. That page covers delivery models, assembly methods, RFQ files and the relationship between sourcing, fabrication and testing.

### Is Turnkey PCBA a separate first-level service?
No. Turnkey PCBA is a delivery model within the PCB Assembly / PCBA service path. It may coordinate PCB fabrication support, approved component sourcing, assembly, inspection, testing and packaging under one reviewed quotation.

### When should I choose EMS & Box Build?
Use EMS & Box Build when the scope extends beyond a board into enclosure, cable or harness, mechanical components, system-level checks, labeling, packaging or delivery preparation.

### Can I request only PCB fabrication support?
A bare-board requirement can be reviewed. The fabrication page also explains why downstream assembly constraints should be considered before the board specification is finalized.

### Are sourcing and testing automatically included?
No. Sourcing responsibilities, alternative-part rules, inspection methods and testing coverage must be confirmed in the quotation and approved project documents.
